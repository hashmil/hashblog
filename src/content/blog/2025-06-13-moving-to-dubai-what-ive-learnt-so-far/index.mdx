  - _template: content
    body: "1.  **_Dubai drivers are a bit wild._** They're not as polite as UK drivers. They can be aggressive. If they flash their headlights, it means \"Get out of the way!\" In the UK, a flash often means \"I'm here\" or \"You can go.\" It's the opposite here. Sometimes I flash to let people in, and they just slow down, trying to let me go. I think the crazy driving is partly because of the **road layouts**. It feels like a toddler made some crayon drawings and they made the road layout out of it. The tricky clover leaf intersections and short lane merges and branches mean you have to be a bit aggressive. As the memes say, \"you miss an exit and will end up in Saudi!\" But, on the good side, it took me only 15 minutes to change my UK driving licence to a UAE one."
  - _template: content
    body: "2.  **_You can't just drink tap water here_.** I still buy bottled water. But you can get big water bottles delivered regularly. Or you can have water filters put on your taps."
  - _template: content
    body: "3.  **_Places stay open late._** Supermarkets, malls, and restaurants often stay open past midnight. This means you can actually go out and do things after work, unlike in the UK."
  - _template: content
    body: "4.  **_But places open late too._** Unlike the UK, most shops open after 10 or 11 AM. If you need something very early, it can be tricky."
  - _template: content
    body: "5.  **_They deliver fuel to your home._** There's a service called [CAFU](https://www.cafu.com/). A green truck comes to your home after you book it with an app. I usually leave my fuel flap open overnight. Then I book them to come and fill it up while I'm asleep."
  - _template: content
    body: "6.  **_There are many food delivery apps._** Talabat, Careem, Noon, Deliveroo, Smile. All these have loads of restaurants and cafes. You can get fast food or healthy meals. Even Keto or low-carb choices are available."
  - _template: content
    body: "7.  **_Service is cheap._** We have a maid once a week and a gardener three times a week. The gardener also washes my car once a week. It's much cheaper than in the UK. Many people in Dubai even have live-in maids or nannies for a good price."
  - _template: content
    body: "8.  **_They have an app for everything._** Going out at night and need a temporary driver to take your car home? There's an app for that. Want a handyman to put up your TV? There's an app for that. Want your trainers cleaned and made new? Yep, there's an app for that too."
  - _template: content
    body: "9.  **_You need an Emirates ID for most things._** This includes renting a home or car, setting up bank accounts, and getting medical insurance. Mine took a month to arrive."
  - _template: content
    body: "10. **_Most things happen on WhatsApp._** It's normal to do business this way. People even share scans of their Emirates ID or passport on WhatsApp."
